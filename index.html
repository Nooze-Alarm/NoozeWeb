The Science Behind Smart Waking

Sleep occurs in cycles, typically lasting 90-110 minutes. Each cycle includes stages of light sleep, deep sleep, and REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep. Waking up during deep sleep can leave you feeling groggy and disoriented, while waking during light sleep or REM results in feeling refreshed and alert.

Nooze uses advanced algorithms to monitor your sleep patterns and detect when you're in a light sleep phase. By waking you at the optimal moment—typically within a 30-minute window before your desired wake time—you'll start your day feeling more energized and ready to take on whatever comes your way.

Key Sleep Cycle Facts:

  • • Most people experience 4-6 sleep cycles per night
  • • Light sleep phases are the best times to wake up naturally
  • • Waking during deep sleep can cause sleep inertia (grogginess)
  • • Consistent sleep schedules help regulate your circadian rhythm
